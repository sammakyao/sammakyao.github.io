  • the swahili google

    Role: project

    Location: Dar es Salaam, Tanzania | First High School Project

    Context
    In Tanzania, out of eleven million students in government schools whose language of instruction is Swahili, only 750,000 students complete their primary education every year. Consequently, many of these students will inevitably face setbacks while transiting to their secondary education studies, where the English language is used for instruction in all schools. The education journey of many Tanzanian kids is thus halted just in the very early stages. Millions of Tanzanian Children who do not gain secondary or vocational training are then forced to engage in child labor, often in exploitative, abusive, or hazardous conditions, to supplement income for their poor families. Therefore, the demand for a friendly Tanzanian English Language teaching aid is rising due to student concerns, education subsidies, and a high number of students.

    Description
    Developed an offline SMS-based search engine using Java to serve over 171,000 vocabulary definitions and translations to Tanzanian students, addressing the educational challenges of transitioning from Swahili to English. The system integrates an SMS modem and SIM card to receive and process requests via text messages, ensuring accessibility without internet connectivity. The backend infrastructure utilizes a repurposed old CPU for data storage and query processing, supported by SQLite for efficient local database management. Additional components include Arduino modules for prototyping, and Processing software for building Java-based executable files to ensure system functionality. The system is powered by Android Studio for further app development to enhance user experience on mobile platforms. This project achieved an 80% improvement in academic performance for 200 students and won the National Young Scientists Tanzania Competition (Technology Category) out of 5,760 entries.

    Tools: java, old-cpu, sqlite, android-studio, arduino modules
